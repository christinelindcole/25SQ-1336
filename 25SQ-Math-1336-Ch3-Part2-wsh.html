Curvature Examples:


Figure 1: Diagram illustrating an osculating circle.

Curvature, denoted κ, quantiﬁes how quickly a curve changes direction at a given point.

κ = ∥dT→ ds ∥ = ∥T→′(t)∥ ∥r→′(t)∥ = ∥r→′(t) ×r→″(t)∥ ∥r→′(t)∥3

 
The osculating circle, shown in the diagram, is the circle that is tangent to the curve at the given point and has the same curvature as the curve at that point. It has radius R = 1 κ.
