Diﬀerential Calculus with Parametric Curves:


Figure 1: Figure showing a line tangent to a parametric curve at a point P.

Consider a point P on a parametric curve C that has coordinates given by

P : (x(t),y(t)).

We would like to extend the concepts of slope and concavity from Diﬀerential Calculus to the new setting of parametric curves.
 
Slope:

dy dx = dy dt dx dt = y′(t) x′(t)

Note that the tangent line will be: horizontal at points where y′(t) = 0,
vertical at points where x′(t) = 0.

To ﬁnd an equation for the tangent line, you can use the the point-slope equation of a line:

y − y1 = m(x − x1)

Concavity:

d2y dx2 = d dt dy dx x′(t)
