Section 3.3: TNB Frame

TNB Frame:

At any point on a smooth curve, r→(t), where T→′(t)≠0→, we can deﬁne the following set of vectors. Note that these vectors all have unit length, and are all mutually orthogonal.


Figure 1: TNB Frame plotted at a point along the helix from the Chapter 3, Part 1 Worksheet.

Unit Tangent Vector:

T→(t) = r→′(t) ∥r→′(t)∥

Unit Normal Vector:

N→(t) = T→′(t) ∥T→′(t)∥

Binormal Vector:

B→(t) = T→(t) ×N→(t)

 
The TNB Frame, also known as the Frenet frame of reference, is the the three-dimensional frame of reference formed by the unit tangent vector, T→, the unit normal vector, N→, and the binormal vector, B→. They form a moving reference frame at any point on a given smooth curve.

Note that the osculating circle lies in the plane determined by the vectors T→ and N→.
