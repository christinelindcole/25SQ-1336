Vector Functions, Space Curves, & Arclength:


Figure 1: Diagram illustrating a general space curve in three dimensions.

A space curve in ℝ3 can be parametrized as a vector function whose tip traces out the curve C:

r→(t) = ⟨f(t),g(t),h(t)⟩

Tangent Vector:

r→′(t) = ⟨f′(t),g′(t),h′(t)⟩

Unit Tangent Vector:

T→(t) = r→′(t) ∥r→′(t)∥

Deﬁnite Integrals:

∫ abr→(t)dt = ⟨∫ abf(t)dt,∫ abg(t)dt,∫ abh(t)dt⟩

Arclength:

L = ∫ ab(dx dt )2 + (dy dt )2 + (dz dt )2dt = ∫ ab∥r→′(t)∥dt
