Cross Product Deﬁnition:

The cross product of two vectors, a→ = a1,a2,a3 ,b→ = b1,b2,b3 results in a vector that is orthogonal to both a→ and b→.
 

a→×b→ = ı ̂ȷ ̂k ̂ a 1 a2 a3 b1 b2 b3 = a2a3 b2 b3 ı ̂−a1a3 b1 b3 ȷ ̂+ a1a2 b1 b2 k ̂ = a2b3 − a3b2 ı ̂−a1b3 − a3b1 ȷ ̂+a1b2 − a2b1 k ̂

 

The direction of the vector a→ ×b→ is determined using the right hand rule, and it’s magnitude is given by ||a→ ×b→|| = ||a→||||b→||sin𝜃, where 𝜃 is the angle between a→ and b→.

Right Hand Rule:


Figure 1: Figure used to demonstrate the right hand rule.

 

To determine the direction of a→ ×b→, point the ﬁngers of your right hand in the direction of a→, then curl them toward b→. Your thumb will then point in the direction of a→ ×b→!

Note that order matters! (See property 1 on the next page.)

Example 1:

Find a→ ×b→ and verify that it is perpendicular to a→ and b→.

a→ = 1,2,3,b→ = 4,5,6

a→×b→ = ı ̂ȷ ̂k ̂ 1 2 3 4 5 6 = 23 5 6 ı ̂−13 4 6 ȷ ̂+ 12 4 5 k ̂ = (2)(6) − (3)(5)ı ̂−(1)(6) − (3)(4)ȷ ̂+(1)(5) − (2)(4)k ̂

a→ ×b→ = 12 − 15ı ̂−6 − 12ȷ ̂ + 5 − 8k ̂ = −3,6,−3

To verify that the result is perpendicular to both a→ and b→, use the dot product!

a→ ×b→ ⋅a→ = −3,6,−3 ⋅1,2,3 = −3 + 12 − 9 = 0

a→ ×b→ ⋅b→ = −3,6,−3 ⋅4,5,6 = −12 + 30 − 18 = 0

Algebraic Properties of the Cross Product:

a→,b→,c→ are all vectors in either ℝ2 or ℝ3, and s is a scalar

1.
a→ ×b→ = −b→ ×a→
2.
(sa→) ×b→ = s(a→ ×b→) = a→ × (sb→)
3.
a→ ×b→ + c→ = a→ ×b→ + a→ ×c→
4.
a→ + b→ ×c→ = a→ ×c→ + b→ ×c→
5.
a→ ⋅b→ ×c→ = a→ ×b→ ⋅c→
6.
a→ ×b→ ×c→ = a→ ⋅c→b→ −a→ ⋅b→c→

Geometric Properties of the Cross Product:


Figure 2: Diagram illustrating the area of a parallelogram determined by vectors a and b.

 

The area of the parallelogram determined by a→ and b→ is given by

Aparallelogram = ||a→ ×b→|| = ||a→||||b→||sin𝜃

The area of the triangle PQR is half of the area of the parallelogram:

Atriangle = 1 2||a→ ×b→||

These areas would be zero if the vectors were parallel:

Vectors a→ and b→ are parallel if-and-only-if a→ ×b→ = 0→.

Torque:


Figure 3: Diagram illustrating applied torque using a ratchet on a bolt under Dr. Cole’s desk.

 

The torque, τ→, from applying a force F→ to a rigid body at a point given by position vector r→ is calculated using:

τ→ = r→ ×F→ ||τ→|| = ||r→||||F→||sin𝜃
||τ→|| = (length of wrench) ∗ (component of force perpendicular to wrench)

 

“righty-tighty, lefty-loosy” rule ⇔ right hand rule for cross products!
